# Venture Capital Analyst

## Definition

An analyst role within venture capital firms focused on sourcing and evaluating early-stage company investments. VC analysts identify promising startups and founders.

## Explanation

VC analysts have two primary objectives:

1. **Deal Sourcing** — identify and source potential deals with companies in early development stages
2. **Founder & Opportunity Assessment** — evaluate business plans and scout for:
   - Revenue growth potential
   - Great entrepreneurs
   - Market opportunity

## Key Skills

The role emphasizes:
- Deal structuring
- Communication with founders
- Business plan evaluation
- Assessment of market potential and founder quality

## Lifestyle

Compared to [[private-equity-analyst|PE analysts]], VC work typically involves:
- Earlier-stage companies (more risk, higher upside)
- Smaller deal sizes
- More founder interaction and mentorship
- Different risk-return expectations

## Career Progression

Entry-level VC analyst → Senior analyst → Associate → Partner

Many VC analysts transition to founding their own companies or moving to other investor roles.

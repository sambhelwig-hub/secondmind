# Private Equity Analyst

## Definition

An analyst role within private equity firms focused on evaluating and acquiring mature companies. PE analysts work on significantly larger deals than their VC counterparts.

## Explanation

PE analysts handle three main activities:

1. **Financial Modeling & Company Evaluation** — build models and analyze potential acquisition targets
2. **Target Research** — research and identify attractive companies to acquire
3. **Post-Deal Support** — work with portfolio companies after acquisition to optimize operations, implement improvements, and prepare for exit

## Deal Characteristics

- Work on significantly larger deals than [[venture-capital-analyst|VC analysts]]
- Typically aim to acquire a majority stake or full ownership of target firms
- Focus on mature, established companies (versus early-stage startups in VC)

## Skill Development

PE analysts develop:
- Advanced financial modeling expertise
- Valuation skills (DCF, comparable companies, precedent transactions)
- Deep operational analysis and improvement strategy
- Post-deal execution and portfolio management

## Career Progression

Entry-level PE analyst → Senior analyst → Associate → Principal/Partner

Many PE analysts move to operating partner roles within their firm or transition to CEO/CFO positions at portfolio companies.

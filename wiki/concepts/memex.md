# Memex

## Definition

A hypothetical personal device for storing, annotating, and linking documents with associative trails, proposed by [[vannevar-bush|Vannevar Bush]] in his 1945 essay "As We May Think."

## Explanation

The Memex was envisioned as a desk-sized device where a user could store all their books, records, and communications on microfilm, then create "trails" linking related items across documents. It was private, actively curated, and the associative links between documents were as valuable as the documents themselves.

The Memex is widely considered a conceptual precursor to hypertext, the web, and personal knowledge management tools. But the web became public and decentralized — quite different from Bush's private, curated vision. The [[llm-wiki-pattern]] is arguably closer to the original Memex than the web is: private, curated, with rich associative structure maintained between documents.

The missing piece in 1945 was who does the maintenance — who builds and keeps the trails current. The answer in 2026: LLMs.

## Examples

- A personal Obsidian vault with LLM-maintained cross-references is a modern Memex.

# Investment Management

## Definition

The field of managing client investment portfolios and funds. Asset managers and investment funds invest client money across various asset classes with the goal of generating returns aligned with the client's risk-return profile.

## Explanation

Asset managers help clients allocate capital across:
- Stocks
- Bonds
- Mutual funds
- Real estate investment trusts (REITs)
- Venture capital funds
- Private equity funds
- Hedge funds
- Alternative assets (art, cryptocurrencies, etc.)

The field includes two major sub-domains: [[venture-capital]] and [[private-equity]].

## Venture Capital (VC)

VC analysts source early-stage company deals, scout for revenue growth potential and great entrepreneurs, and evaluate business plans. The role emphasizes deal structuring and communication with founders.

## Private Equity (PE)

PE analysts work on significantly larger deals. They typically aim to acquire a majority stake or full ownership of mature companies. PE analysts build financial models, evaluate targets, and provide substantial post-deal support to the companies they acquire.

# Traditional Banking

## Definition

The historic core of banking: deposit-taking from customers and lending to individuals and businesses. Modern banks also offer digital banking, forex, corporate banking, insurance, and wealth management.

## Explanation

Traditional banks play an intermediary role — they take deposits from savers and lend money to borrowers, earning a spread between deposit rates and lending rates.

## Key Roles

**Credit Analyst**
- Assesses creditworthiness of loan applicants (individuals and businesses)
- Analyzes financial statements, credit reports, and other data
- Determines lending risk and recommends whether the bank should lend

**Risk Analyst (Entry-level Risk Management)**
- Develops financial models
- Evaluates the bank's exposures across its portfolio
- Proposes strategies to mitigate risks
- Manages the large amounts of capital the bank deploys

**Relationship Manager**
- Works directly with corporate clients
- Builds relationships and fosters client loyalty
- Provides insights into client businesses
- Develops soft skills through interaction with executives

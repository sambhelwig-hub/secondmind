# Corporate Finance

## Definition

Finance roles and functions within non-financial corporations (e.g., Coca-Cola, Nike, Starbucks). Involves managing the company's cash, investments, capital structure, and financial planning.

## Explanation

Corporate finance roles fall into two main categories:

**Accounting/Financial Control:**
- Accountants — maintain financial records
- Accounts receivable specialists — manage customer payments
- Accounts payable specialists — manage supplier payments
- Internal auditors — ensure compliance, safeguard against fraud

**Financial Analysis:**
- Financial analysts — study financial performance, provide insights to management
- Financial controller — calculates product/service costs
- FP&A (Financial Planning & Analysis) team — forecasts revenue, costs, and balance sheet items

## Career Progression

Entry-level → Financial Analyst → Finance Manager → Director → VP → CFO

## Examples

- Analyzing quarterly earnings and providing insights to executives
- Forecasting cash flow for the next 12 months
- Calculating manufacturing costs per unit
- Managing accounts payable to ensure suppliers are paid on time

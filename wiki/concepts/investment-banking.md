# Investment Banking

## Definition

Financial advisory and capital markets services offered to corporations and financial institutions. Investment banks help clients raise capital, structure mergers and acquisitions, and execute trading operations.

## Explanation

Investment banks serve three main functions:

1. **Capital Raising** — help companies issue debt or equity to raise money
2. **M&A Advisory** — advise on mergers, acquisitions, and restructuring
3. **Trading** — buy and sell securities for themselves or clients

Entry-level roles include:
- Equity or debt capital markets analyst
- M&A analyst
- Trader
- Investment researcher

## The Appeal

The field is "challenging and rewarding" and favored by top candidates for its dynamic career development. Many finance graduates dream of being M&A analysts — the thrill of closing high-stakes deals and working on transformative transactions.

## M&A Analyst Role

M&A analysts identify acquisition targets, build financial models, value businesses, and conduct due diligence — investigating the target's financial, operational, and strategic aspects to identify risks and opportunities.

## Career Progression

Associate → VP → Director → Managing Director

(Due to the industry's rigorous demands, fewer individuals stay at each level. Many analysts move to [[private-equity]] or [[venture-capital]] after a few years.)

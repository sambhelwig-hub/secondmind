# M&A Analyst

## Definition

An analyst role within investment banking focused on mergers and acquisitions. M&A analysts identify acquisition targets, perform financial analysis, value businesses, and conduct due diligence.

## Explanation

M&A analysts handle three main activities:

1. **Target Identification** — scout potential acquisition targets
2. **Financial Modeling & Valuation** — build models to value companies and analyze deal economics
3. **Due Diligence** — investigate and verify the target's financial, operational, and strategic aspects to identify risks and opportunities

## Why It's Coveted

"The dream of many finance graduates" — the appeal is:
- The thrill of closing high-stakes deals
- Working on transformative transactions that hit headlines
- Deepening understanding of business strategy and financial structuring
- Working with real, complex businesses and executives

## Skill Development

M&A analysts develop:
- Advanced financial modeling skills
- Valuation expertise
- Business analysis and strategic thinking
- Client and stakeholder management

## Career Progression

Entry-level M&A analyst → Senior analyst → Associate → VP → Director → Managing Director

Many M&A analysts move to [[private-equity]] after a few years of investment banking experience.

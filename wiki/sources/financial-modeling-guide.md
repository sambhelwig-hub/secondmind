## Summary

Comprehensive beginner's guide to financial modeling fundamentals. Financial Edge explains what financial modeling is, where it's used, the different types of models, and the detailed steps for building a three-statement model in Excel. The guide emphasizes that financial modeling involves building financial statements to represent company performance using historical data, calculated ratios, forecast assumptions, and linked calculations.

The source provides practical, step-by-step instructions for building models, from initial data input through final balance sheet reconciliation. It also covers best practices for model maintenance, editing, and documentation.

## Key Takeaways

- **Definition:** Financial modeling is building financial statements to represent a company's financial performance using historical data, ratios, assumptions, and forecasts.
- **Purpose:** Models serve as the foundation for investment decisions, performance analysis, and future forecasting.
- **10 Common Model Types:** Three-Statement, Forecast, LBO, DCF, M&A, Sum-of-the-Parts, Comparable Company, IPO, Divestiture, and Option Pricing models.
- **Three-Statement Model is the foundation:** All other models build on the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement.
- **10-Step Building Process:** From preparation and historical data input to interest calculation and balance sheet reconciliation.
- **Key Skills:** Ratio analysis, assumption-building, Excel formulas, circular reference management, and dependency tracking.
- **Best Practices:** Save backups, turn off iteration settings, make one edit at a time, label clearly, trace dependencies, and keep the model clean.
- **Excel Technical Skills:** Understanding circular references, the MAX and MIN functions for cash/debt plugging, and formula dependencies.
